\noindent\textbf{Justification for Each Dimension:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Software Development \& Programming (Level 4):} The system requires intricate state management processing four camera streams concurrently with $49.4$ ms latency, proving scalable architecture capability beyond simple applications.
    \item \textbf{Machine Learning \& AI (Level 4):} The combination of OSNet-AIN, ByteTrack, and YOLO models into a cohesive pipeline demonstrates advanced mastery of custom AI implementations.
    \item \textbf{Data Management \& Analytics (Level 3):} Managing the embeddings (512-dimensional arrays) via cosine-similarity dictionaries in real-time aligns with Level 3 real-time data pipelines.
    \item \textbf{Critical \& Creative Thinking (Level 4):} Proposing the "edge trigger" heuristic and homography tracking to bypass the high overhead of continuous Re-ID marks an innovative optimization logic.
    \item \textbf{Collaboration \& Communication (Level 3):} The IEEE structured reporting and systematic documentation confirm a well-structured technical communication capability.
    \item \textbf{Lifelong Learning (Level 4):} Successfully researching, understanding, and integrating a complex mix of new computer vision algorithms (homography) and neural networks (OSNet, YOLO) demonstrates an exceptional capacity for independent and continuous learning.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Reflection on the AI Evaluation}

The AI evaluation provided a fair and insightful assessment of the technical competencies demonstrated in the project. It accurately identified strengths in software architecture and machine learning integration. The AI correctly highlighted the novelty of using homography for overlapping views combined with Re-ID only on camera edge-zones as a primary strength in "Critical \& Creative Thinking." We believe the assessment is highly reasonable, although we would argue our data management required heavier algorithmic lifting behind the scenes than a strict Level 3 suggests. Despite this, it was not overly strict nor too lenient, as it maintained focus on the provided source code.

\subsection{Student Reflection on the Advisor’s Evaluation}
Reviewing the advisor's evaluation provides a holistic and grounding perspective on the project's achievements. We strongly agree with the advisor’s high ratings in Machine Learning \& AI, Critical Thinking, and Communication (Level 4). The advisor’s acknowledgment of the significant performance improvements achieved by integrating YOLO, ByteTrack, and OSNet-AIN validates the core technical innovations of the system. Furthermore, matching our self-evaluation of 4 in Communication highlights the success of our continuous user feedback loop and active knowledge-sharing in research lab meetings, which the isolated AI-based assessment could not fully observe.

However, there is a notable, yet highly constructive, divergence in the areas of Software Development and Lifelong Learning, where the advisor assigned a Level 3, compared to the Level 4 ratings from both the AI and our self-evaluation. For Software Development, while the AI focused on strict backend latency optimizations and algorithm complexity, the advisor correctly viewed the project through an enterprise lens---taking into account the broader requirements of UX/UI design, testing procedures, and containerization. This reveals a vital lesson: building a robust tracking pipeline is only one component of delivering a fully matured, production-ready software product. Similarly, for Lifelong Learning, the advisor's Level 3 rating serves as a realistic academic benchmark. While we successfully assimilated complex computer vision paradigms for this specific project, achieving true expert-level lifelong learning (Level 4) requires a sustained, career-long commitment to adapting technologies across diverse industry applications.

Ultimately, the advisor's assessment acts as an invaluable bridge between academic research and industry expectations. It validates our technical and collaborative competencies while clarifying our long-term professional development goals: elevating our end-to-end software engineering practices (such as rigorous testing architectures and advanced MLOps) to the same high standards we apply to machine learning research.
