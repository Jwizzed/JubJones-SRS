\chapter{Knowledge and Skills Evaluation}

This chapter evaluates the knowledge and skills developed throughout the project by comparing them with the Program Learning Outcomes (PLOs) and the expected rubric levels established by the curriculum. The evaluation is conducted from three perspectives: AI-based assessment, student self-assessment, and advisor assessment. The purpose of this chapter is not only to measure achievement, but also to provide reflective insight into how different evaluative perspectives reveal strengths, gaps, and opportunities for further development.

\section{Comparison Between Program Targets and AI-Based Evaluation}

\textbf{Justification for Each Dimension:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Software Development \& Programming (Level 4):} The system requires intricate state management processing four camera streams concurrently with $49.4$ ms latency, proving scalable architecture capability beyond simple applications.
    \item \textbf{Machine Learning \& AI (Level 4):} The combination of OSNet-AIN, ByteTrack, and YOLO models into a cohesive pipeline demonstrates advanced mastery of custom AI implementations.
    \item \textbf{Data Management \& Analytics (Level 3):} Managing the embeddings (512-dimensional arrays) via cosine-similarity dictionaries in real-time aligns with Level 3 real-time data pipelines.
    \item \textbf{Critical \& Creative Thinking (Level 4):} Proposing the "edge trigger" heuristic and homography tracking to bypass the high overhead of continuous Re-ID marks an innovative optimization logic.
    \item \textbf{Collaboration \& Communication (Level 3):} The IEEE structured reporting and systematic documentation confirm a well-structured technical communication capability.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Reflection on the AI Evaluation}
This section discusses the student’s opinions regarding the AI’s assessment. The AI evaluation provided a fair and insightful assessment of the technical competencies demonstrated in the project. It accurately identified strengths in software architecture and machine learning integration. The AI correctly highlighted the novelty of using homography for overlapping views combined with Re-ID only on camera edge-zones as a primary strength in "Critical \& Creative Thinking." I believe the assessment is highly reasonable, although I would argue my data management required heavier algorithmic lifting behind the scenes than a strict Level 3 suggests. Despite this, it was not overly strict nor too lenient, as it maintained focus on the provided source code.

\subsection{Reflection on Self-Evaluation Compared with AI Evaluation}
This section presents the interpretation of the self-evaluation in comparison with the AI assessment. When comparing the self-evaluation to the AI, I agreed with the high marks across all technical aspects, scoring myself equal to the AI in Software Development, Machine Learning, Data Management, and Critical Thinking. However, I rated myself a level higher in Communication. This discrepancy stems from the fact that the AI only sees the final, polished code and documentation, completely missing the extensive on-site stakeholder communication required to evaluate the camera grids inside the actual factory logic and the countless offline team discussions held to iteratively debug our system.
